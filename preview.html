Pasta al pomodoro

A quick and light dish, garnished with fresh basil, parsley and a drizzle of pesto.

Ingredients

  • 400 grams
    Dried Linguine
  • 250 grams
    Cherry Tomatoes
    Halved
  • 2 Onions
    Finely chopped
  • 4 cloves of Garlic (crushed)
  • 500 grams
    Passata
    recipe in book
  • 100 grams
    Green Pesto
    recipe in book
  • 100 grams
    grated Italian hard cheese
  • 200 grams
    Creme Fraiche
  • 1 tablespoon
    Olive Oil
  • 4 tablespoons
    Basil leaves (roughly chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons
    Balsamic Vinegar
  • To taste
    Sea Salt
    Black Pepper

Instructions

Make the Pasta

Bring a large pot of salted water to a roiling boil and add the linguine when boiling. After 12 minutes, remove the pot from the heat and drain the water then add a drizzle of olive oil to prevent the pasta from sticking together.

Prepare the vegetables

Cut the cherry tomatoes in half and place them in a small bowl, sprinkle a pinch of salt and sugar on them and set them aside.
Finely dice the onion, sprinkle them with a small bit of water.
Chop the garlic very finely and crush them with the flat side of your kitchen knife, or use a garlic press, mix the garlic with your finely diced onion and set them both aside.
Roughly chop the basil leaves.

Make the Pasta Sauce

Place a frying pan on the heat and add a small glug of olive oil. Add the garlic and onion mix into the pan once hot and allow to soften for 3-4 minutes while stiring.
Once the onion and garlic has softened you can add the halved cherry tomatoes and chopped basil to the pan. Add some pepper and allow to soften for about 6-8 minutes.
Add the creme fraiche, balsamic vinegar and passata to the vegetables and allow it to thicken for about 5 minutes.
Taste the sauce as it has thickened and add some salt and pepper if needed. Carefully add the pasta with some tongs and evenly coat with the sauce.

Serve and Enjoy!

Divide the pasta onto plates and drizzle over the green pesto, lastly sprinkle over the grated cheese E ora si mangia! Enjoy!
Tip: Serve with a toasted slice of bread or baguette.
